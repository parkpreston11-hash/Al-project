Build a professional, premium website for a person who is both a real estate agent and a loan officer.

The website should look modern, trustworthy, clean, and high-end, but the backend should stay simple and reliable. Do not build anything overly complicated that could easily break. Focus on a polished front-end, simple lead forms, and a basic admin dashboard.

Main goal:
The website should help visitors view homes, learn about buying/selling/financing, and contact the agent/loan officer.

Brand message:
“One trusted professional for buying, selling, and financing your home.”

Design style:
Use a luxury real estate style with white, dark navy, charcoal, soft gray, and subtle gold accents. Use clean spacing, modern fonts, rounded cards, smooth hover effects, professional icons, and high-quality home imagery. Make the site fully mobile responsive.

Pages to create:

1. Home Page

Create a strong hero section with:

* Professional photo placeholder
* Luxury home background image
* Headline: “Buy, Sell, and Finance Your Home With Confidence”
* Subheadline: “Work with one trusted professional for real estate guidance and home financing support.”
* Buttons:
    * View Homes
    * Start My Home Buying Plan
    * Contact Me

Add sections for:

* Featured Listings
* Recently Sold Homes
* Loan Programs
* Why Work With Me
* Simple Home Buying Roadmap
* Testimonials
* Contact call-to-action

2. Current Listings Page

Create a page that shows homes currently for sale.

Each listing card should include:

* Property photo
* Price
* Address
* Beds
* Baths
* Square feet
* Short description
* View Details button

Keep the listings editable from simple sample data. Do not connect to MLS or outside APIs.

3. Property Details Page

When someone clicks a listing, show:

* Large image gallery
* Price
* Address
* Beds, baths, square feet
* Property description
* Features and amenities
* Estimated monthly payment section
* Contact / Schedule Showing button

Estimated payment should be simple and only for display. Include this disclaimer:
“Estimated payment is for educational purposes only and does not represent final loan terms or approval.”

4. Recently Sold Page

Create a clean gallery of recently sold homes.

Each sold property should show:

* Photo
* Sold badge
* Address
* Sold price
* Beds, baths, square feet
* Short success description

This page should build trust and show proof of experience.

5. Sell Your Home Page

Create a page for seller leads.

Headline:
“Thinking About Selling Your Home?”

Include sections:

* Why sell with this agent
* Local market knowledge
* Professional marketing
* Negotiation support
* Smooth closing process

Add a simple form:

* Full name
* Phone number
* Email
* Property address
* Timeline to sell
* Message

Button text:
“Request My Home Value”

6. Loan Officer Page

Create a professional page explaining the loan officer side.

Headline:
“Home Financing Made Simple”

Explain that visitors can get guidance on possible loan options, monthly payment estimates, and the home buying process.

Include loan program cards:

* FHA Loans
* Conventional Loans
* VA Loans
* Jumbo Loans
* Refinance Options
* Investment Property Loans

Each card should have:

* Simple explanation
* Who it may be good for
* Contact button

Do not promise approval, rates, or exact terms.

7. Mortgage Calculator Page

Create a simple mortgage calculator with:

* Home price
* Down payment
* Interest rate
* Loan term

Display estimated monthly payment.

Include disclaimer:
“This calculator provides estimates only. Actual payment, rate, and loan terms may vary.”

8. Home Buying Plan Form

Create a simple lead form, not a full loan application.

Fields:

* Full name
* Phone number
* Email
* Buying or refinancing?
* Desired price range
* Estimated down payment range
* Credit score range
* Desired purchase timeline
* Additional comments

Button text:
“Start My Home Buying Plan”

After submission, show:
“Thank you. Your information has been received. A professional will contact you soon.”

Add disclaimer:
“This is not a loan application or approval. Information submitted is used so a licensed professional can contact you to discuss possible options.”

9. About Page

Create a professional about page with:

* Headshot placeholder
* Biography
* Real estate experience
* Loan officer experience
* Areas served
* Mission statement
* Contact button

Make the tone trustworthy, confident, and personal.

10. Contact Page

Include:

* Phone number
* Email address
* Office location
* Business hours
* Contact form
* Map placeholder

11. Admin Dashboard

Create a simple password-protected admin dashboard.

The dashboard should allow the owner to:

* View leads from forms
* View buyer leads
* View seller leads
* View home buying plan submissions
* Search leads
* Sort leads by newest
* Change lead status
* Add notes to leads
* Export leads to CSV

Lead statuses:

* New Lead
* Contacted
* Appointment Scheduled
* Active Client
* Closed

Keep this dashboard simple. Do not add complex CRM features.

Important backend instructions:

* Do not use email automation.
* Do not connect to MLS.
* Do not connect to credit checks.
* Do not add document uploads.
* Do not add payment processing.
* Do not add complicated loan approval logic.
* Store form submissions in a simple database.
* Use sample listings that can be edited manually.
* Prioritize stability over complex features.

Footer:
Include:

* Navigation links
* Contact info
* Social media placeholders
* Licensing information placeholder
* Equal Housing Opportunity text
* Privacy Policy
* Terms of Service

Overall feeling:
The website should feel like a premium real estate and mortgage website, but be built in a simple, stable way that will not easily break.
